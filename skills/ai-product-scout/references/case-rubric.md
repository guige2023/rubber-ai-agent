# Product Case Rubric

Use this rubric when the user wants productization or commercialization case studies rather than a simple product list.

## 1. Case Selection

Choose cases that have the strongest combination of:

- `Clear Problem`: the user problem is legible
- `Visible Productization`: the product turns a messy workflow into something easier, faster, or more repeatable
- `Commercial Clues`: there are signs of pricing, monetization, or buyer intent
- `Distribution Clues`: there is some evidence of how users find or adopt it
- `Learnability`: a builder can extract an actionable lesson

If a product is interesting but too thin to support those dimensions, keep it in the shortlist but do not force it into the featured case slot.

## 2. Productization Questions

For the featured case, answer as many of these as the evidence supports:

- What exact user problem is being solved?
- Which workflow is being compressed?
- What is the time-to-value for a new user?
- What part of the experience appears most differentiated?
- Why does this look like a product, not a demo?

## 3. Commercialization Questions

Look for evidence or reasonable clues about:

- Who pays
- What the pricing or packaging appears to be
- Whether the product feels self-serve, PLG, sales-led, or hybrid
- Where expansion might come from
- What kind of moat or switching cost may exist

When the evidence is incomplete, write carefully and mark it as interpretation.

## 4. Distribution Questions

Try to infer how the product gets attention:

- content
- community
- launch platforms
- search
- developer ecosystem
- partnerships
- enterprise sales

You do not need certainty, but you do need a grounded explanation.

## 5. Writing Quality

A good case study should do all of the following:

- teach the reader something concrete
- explain why the case matters
- distinguish fact from interpretation
- end with clear lessons for builders

## 6. Chinese Publishing Finish

For Chinese outputs:

- remove non-essential spaces between Chinese and adjacent English or numbers,
- preserve literal formatting only where needed,
- make the result read like native Chinese editorial writing.
